==================================================

NAME
  Reel Audio Fixer

SUMMARY  (AMO max ~250 characters)
  Instagram Reels leak an audio stream for every video you scroll past, until
  Firefox runs out and your sound dies. Reel Audio Fixer bounds the leak so your
  audio keeps working — no matter how long you scroll.

CATEGORY
  Photos, Music & Videos        (alternate: Other)

TAGS
  instagram, reels, audio, sound, video, performance, memory

DATA COLLECTION  (AMO submission asks this)
  Does not collect any data. The extension runs entirely on the page and
  sends nothing anywhere — no network requests, no storage of user data.

DESCRIPTION
  Scrolling Instagram Reels in Firefox slowly kills your sound.

  Instagram opens a new audio stream for every Reel you scroll past and never
  closes them. They pile up as idle streams until Firefox can't open new ones —
  and your audio stops working until you restart the browser. It's a real,
  long-standing bug in Firefox's audio engine (Mozilla bugs 1602345 and
  1851747) and it affects every Firefox-based browser.

  Reel Audio Fixer bounds the leak. As you scroll, it keeps the last few Reels
  behind you alive and releases the audio streams of older ones, so the number
  of open streams plateaus instead of climbing without limit. Your sound keeps
  working no matter how long you scroll.

  - Works silently in the background — no setup, no configuration.
  - Never touches the Reel you're watching or Reels ahead, so playback and
    forward scrolling are never interrupted.
  - Keeps a 5-Reel buffer, so scrolling back a little still works instantly.

  Trade-off: if you scroll back more than 5 Reels, the older ones show gray and
  need a page reload. This is deliberate — infinite scroll is a forward
  experience, and a small buffer keeps memory use bounded.
